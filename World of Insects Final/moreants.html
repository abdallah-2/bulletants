More Ant Species

Explore 10 Popular Ant Species

1. Argentine Ant (Linepithema humile)

The Argentine ant is a highly invasive species known for forming supercolonies. It's small and light to dark brown in color.

2. Army Ant (Eciton spp.)

Army ants are known for their nomadic and aggressive behavior. They travel in large groups and are usually red or brown in color.

3. Carpenter Ant (Camponotus spp.)

Carpenter ants are known for tunneling into wood. They vary in size and are often black, red, or a combination of colors.

4. Red Imported Fire Ant (Solenopsis invicta)

These aggressive ants are known for their painful stings. They are reddish-brown and build large mounds in the ground.

5. Weaver Ant (Oecophylla spp.)

Weaver ants are skilled builders, known for creating complex nests by weaving leaves together. They are reddish-brown to black.

6. Harvester Ant (Pogonomyrmex spp.)

Harvester ants are known for collecting seeds and are often reddish-brown or black. They are found in arid regions.

7. Odorous House Ant (Tapinoma sessile)

These ants emit a rotten coconut-like odor when crushed. They are brown to black and commonly invade homes.

8. Bull Ant (Myrmecia spp.)

Bull ants are large and aggressive ants found in Australia. They can be red, black, or brown and have powerful mandibles.

9. Leafcutter Ant (Atta spp. and Acromyrmex spp.)

Leafcutter ants are known for cutting and farming fungus on leaves. They are brown and found in the Americas.

10. Jack Jumper Ant (Myrmecia pilosula)

Jack jumper ants, native to Australia, are known for their powerful stings and aggressive behavior. They come in various colors, including black and orange.

Did You Know?

There are over 12,000 species of ants! They can be found almost everywhere across the globe with the exception of Antarctica, Iceland, Greenland, and some island nations.
